## Why LabelDeck?

In a time when large language models are widely used, manual labeling is often seen as â€œwork that will soon disappear.â€  
But in reality:

- Many students and researchers **cannot afford LLM APIs**
- Many projects **have no budget and no engineering support**
- Many studies still require **human-generated ground truth**
- Many social science students and research assistants are still doing large amounts of **repetitive, low-visibility labeling work (dirty work)**

This work is rarely seen as prestigious, and it is rarely discussed,  
yet it continues to support research, datasets, and even the models themselves.

**In many contexts, manual labeling remains the only viable option.**

LabelDeck aims to **reduce the additional burden** brought by this unavoidable repetitive work.

## How does it work?

<img width="2852" height="1506" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/136fa093-2def-4914-a33a-732b2dd694b2" />

LabelDeck turns each item to be labeled (text, images, etc.) into a **card**, and:

- Only **one card** is shown at a time
- Items are assigned to categories using **keyboard keys**
- All labeled content can always be revisited and edited

> **When too many items are presented at once, people become more tired, distracted, and prone to mistakes.**

By breaking the labeling task into one card at a time,  
LabelDeck tries to reduce **cognitive load** during labeling:

- No need to scroll back and forth in spreadsheets
- Focus only on the current judgment

This makes the labeling process more direct and easier to sustain over time.

## Technology & Privacy

- All operations run **entirely locally**
- No backend, no server connection
- No third-party APIs
- No data upload or data collection

This makes LabelDeck suitable for:

- Student assignments
- Daily labeling work by research assistants
- Projects with limited budgets or sensitive data
